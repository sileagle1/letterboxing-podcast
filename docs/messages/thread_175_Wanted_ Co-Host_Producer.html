Wanted: Co-Host/Producer

From: johnoballou (johnjballou@hotmail.com) | Date: 2008-12-17 15:31:56 UTC
It makes me sad that I have been pretty swamped this fall with work,
and starting my own business, and haven't had as much time to devote
to getting Letterpod episodes on air. My sincerest apologies to all
the Letterpod fans out there.

I would like to try to fix this problem by posting this open
invitation for a co-host/producer for the Letterpod.

I am happy to announce that I am an expecting father, and lord
willing, will be up late nights beginning in June. So I can for see
that starting in June I may also have less time to devote to the
Letterpod.

However, I do NOT want to abandon it. It's too much fun. However, I
would like some fresh blood poured into the show (that sounds kind of
gruesome actually).

So, if there is anyone out there willing to produce some shows, and
serve as host, I would love to trade off these duties. Maybe we can
do alternating shows, or however it works out.

The basics are this, the co-host/producer would need some editing
software...I use Garageband on the MAC, but Audacity is a free program
that might work for you on a PC or MAC. You will need a
computer/laptop that has a microphone, or one you can plug in. That's
about it! Most of what I do is collect audio files from others, put
them together, add some music (which is all really simple in
Garageband) and that's a show. I do record some phone calls for
interview and segments, and that set-up isn't simple, but if something
like that is needed, I certainly can do that, and send the
co-host/producer an audio file of it.

I will pass along passwords and whatnot to Podbean.com, so you can
learn how to post to the web page.

Anyway, I know it sound like a lot, but with sharing these duties with
me, it probably won't be so rough. I'll guide you every step of the way.

Anyway, if you are interested in taking an active role in the
Letterpod like this, shoot me an email so I can answer any questions
you might have.

Thanks!
jackbear
