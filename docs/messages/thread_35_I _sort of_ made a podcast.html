From: Diana Newton Wood (diana@kjsl.com) | Date: 2006-06-19 09:04:53 UTC-04:00
So I went to plant my Brues Woods Series in Sudbury.
It is an interpretive trail, nicely maintained, with 6 numbered posts.
I came there at dusk, so the birds were trilling.
My idea was to record while I was planting. I had the brochure, so I
was reading the interpretive comments as I went.

The main problem was that this device records in .wav which I know is
convertible to MP3, but is a very bulky format. I didn't have enough
memory to finish the whole thing at once. I am getting a 1G miniSD on
Wednesday, so I think I am going to redo it. My clues include the
interpretive trail, as you enter at station 1 and find the boxes after
station six.
I think it would be nice to offer as an alternative to carrying around
a map and brochure on the clue page, by reading the interpretive trail
markers, and then the clues, as I am going, but it will be better the
second time (I hadn't even been there before last Friday).
