From: Becky G (rgutin@gmail.com) | Date: 2008-02-29 20:21:08 UTC
I would highly recommend that you mail each CD in a plastic jewel
case, then put it in a padded envelope. It is possible to get jewel
cases that will hold 2 CDs at a time.

I mail CDs all the time, and if you don't double protect them, they
WILL break in transit. It costs a little more up front for the
supplies and the additional postage, but they will arrive unbroken.

Just my two cents!
BeckyG

One more thought. If you do end up selling cds, on the order form, or
whatever you use, have a place for people to put their Donation
amount.... basically, you could charge "actual" cost... $1 or
whatever, but most people, I THINK!, will feel guilty about only
paying that much. So be sure to have a place for them to "up" the
amount they pay if they want to. I think a lot of people would be
very happy to pay $5 (for example) if you charge $1.

I go to a Recycle for Education place that charges .30/lb for the
stuff they have (really GREAT donated stuff that is good for office
supplies and art projects). But when you check out and the guy says
you owe $2 for the 3 stuffed grocery bags of goodies you are buying,
he words it "That's $2 or whatever you'd like to donate".

Just my thoughts:  If these are given away free then I wonder who IS going to end up paying for the CDs, padded envelope & postage?  Someone has to pay for this.  I don't think it's right to expect jackbear to cover the costs.  There's no way to know if the demand will be small, medium or large.  Even though I've heard all of the podcasts, I may enjoy having one myself, as may other podcast members.

I like the idea of making these available on CD.  As more podcasts are recorded more CD choices could be made available.  Example:  CD #1 - Podcasts  1-5.  CD #2 - Podcasts 6-10.  CD #3 - Podcasts 11-15.  etc.
