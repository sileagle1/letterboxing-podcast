> No worries, gone are the days of popping in a cassette and hitting

> Yeah, its more complicated now, but its not that much harder. First
> check out this new page I started to see if it helps..
> http://letterpod.podbean.com/podcasting-basics/
>
> Like Orion said, basically you are now recording digitally, instead of
> to a tape. The sound is converted to a digital file, which when
> played back sounds like sound. So how do you record digitally. Well,
> lots of ways. Maybe your laptop or computer has a built in microphone
> already. If not, you might need a mic. As Orion said, you can get
> one that plugs directly into your computer using USB.
>
> Or, you might have an mp3 player or iPod that can record, or do as
> orion does and has an add-on to record. There are high end digital
> recorders too. Maybe you have a MiniDisc recorder, that can work to.
>
> Or, if you have none of these, a quick and dirty way is to go to
> www.gabcast.com and set up a free account. You can call a free phone
> number and record yourself. It will create a digital file for you.
> The sound quality sounds like you are on a phone, but you will have
> something!
>
> Then the next step is taking that file and cleaning and editing it up
> a bit in an audio editing program like Garageband, or Audacity (which
> is a freeware program online for PC and Mac's). If this part
> indimidates you, give the file to me and I'll work on it for you.
>
> Then we post away! I'll do that. So, if you have an idea, run with it
> and we'll figure out a way to use it. Let me know what you do and
> don't have and I'll try to help however I can.
