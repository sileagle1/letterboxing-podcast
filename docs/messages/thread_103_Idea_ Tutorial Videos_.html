Idea? Tutorial Videos?

From: johnoballou (johnjballou@hotmail.com) | Date: 2007-10-17 13:56:47 UTC
Maybe this is off topic or out of the mission of Letterpod, however I
am compelled to share it to see what others think.

The Fright Night video is and has been the most
downloaded/feeded/streamed file on the Letterpod so far. This is
probably no surprise, since more americans watch TV, as opposed to
listen to the radio. People like to "see" things as well as listen to
them. The funny/strange part is that this file is also the LARGEST,
and takes longer to load then the other stuff. Anyway...

Because video is so popular, I am compelled to accept video. I
suppose we could host gathering video (certainly if it was as well
produced as the fright night video), video that are clues to
letterboxes, and finally to my original idea....tutorial videos.

I think DIY is very popular in letterboxing, and I think people are
interested in how others do things. I don't know if these would be
redundant though....I mean there are some great web tutorials out
there already on carving a stamp, I'm not sure a video would improve
on this or just take up bandwidth. But for newer letterboxers, seeing
a video on how to transfer with tracing paper, versus acetone, versus
blender pen, or with an iron, or buther paper, or whatever, might be
very useful. I know I have never really seen the iron thing.

I think that there might be more video camera and web cameras out
there then microphones, so this might be really cool way for folks to
contribute.

So what do you all think, should we advertise this kind of thing? I
can certainly do a sample so everybody could see the potential (I just
watched an online tutorial yesterday on iMovie, and it would be super
easy).

jackbear

If you want to keep to the audio podcasts as the main focus, maybe you could just do topical videos every so often. Like your suggestions on carving - do a whole video podcast of carving clips or such. Or gatherings. Or containers. Or how to hide a letterbox.
